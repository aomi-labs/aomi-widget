# Getting Started

An Aomi assistant is an AI that understands a product's features inside and out. It can look up data, answer your questions, and take actions on your behalf -- all through a simple chat interface.

When a company integrates Aomi into their product, you get a personal AI assistant that knows that product's APIs, documentation, and capabilities. Instead of navigating menus and dashboards, you just describe what you need in plain language.

## What Can You Do With It?

Aomi assistants are configured per product, so the exact capabilities depend on what the product supports. Common things you can do:

- **Ask questions** -- get explanations about features, fees, supported assets, or anything the product covers.
- **Get real-time data** -- ask for token prices, portfolio balances, yield rates, or any live data the product provides.
- **Execute transactions** -- swap tokens, place trades, transfer funds, or perform other actions the product supports.
- **Manage your portfolio** -- check holdings, review transaction history, track performance.
- **Search documentation** -- the assistant can search through docs and knowledge bases the product has uploaded.

You don't need to know which specific API to call or what parameters it takes. Just describe what you want, and the assistant figures out the rest.

## The Chat Experience

Using an Aomi assistant feels like a conversation. You type a message, and the assistant streams its response in real time -- text appears word by word as it thinks.

When the assistant needs to look something up or perform an action, it calls a "tool." Tools are connections to the product's APIs. You'll see these tool calls happen inline in the chat:

- A brief indicator shows what the assistant is doing (e.g., "Getting ETH price..." or "Checking your balance...")
- The result appears right in the conversation
- The assistant then continues its response using that data

This all happens seamlessly. You ask a question, the assistant gathers the information it needs, and gives you a complete answer.

## Threads

Conversations are organized into threads. Each thread is a separate conversation with its own history.

- **Create new threads** for new topics or tasks.
- **Switch between threads** to pick up where you left off.
- **Rename threads** to keep things organized.
- **Delete threads** you no longer need.

Threads persist across sessions, so you can close the chat and come back later to continue a conversation.

## Wallet Connection

Some Aomi assistants support connecting a crypto wallet. This is completely optional, but it unlocks additional capabilities:

- The assistant can query your on-chain balances and transaction history.
- It can prepare transactions for you to review and approve.
- You can interact with DeFi protocols, swap tokens, and manage on-chain assets through the chat.

**Your wallet stays secure.** The assistant never has access to your private keys. When a transaction is needed, the assistant prepares it and sends it to your wallet app for approval. You must explicitly approve every transaction before it executes.

If you don't connect a wallet, the assistant still works -- it can answer questions, look up data, and perform any non-wallet actions.

## Where to Use It

Aomi assistants are available through two channels:

### Web Chat Widget

The most common way to interact with an Aomi assistant. The chat widget is embedded directly in the product's website. Look for a chat icon or a chat panel on the page. It works right in your browser with no installation needed.

### Telegram Bot

Some products also offer their Aomi assistant as a Telegram bot. You can chat with it in direct messages or add it to group chats. The Telegram bot supports the same AI capabilities as the web chat, plus wallet connectivity through a Telegram mini-app.
